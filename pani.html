HOW TO MAKE THE PANNI POORI AND THEIR INGREDIENTS LISTED ARE

Making pani puri (also known as golgappa or puchka) is a delightful culinary adventure! Here's a simple recipe along with the ingredients you'll need:

Ingredients:

For the Puri (outer shell):

  • Semolina (Sooji/Rava):
    • 1 cup
  • All-purpose flour (Maida):
    • 1/4 cup
  • Baking soda:
    • a pinch
  • Salt:
    • a pinch
  • Water:
    • as needed for dough
  • Oil:
    • for deep frying

    For the Pani (spicy water):

  • Mint leaves:
    • 1 cup
  • Coriander leaves:
    • 1/2 cup
  • Green chilies:
    • 2-3 (adjust according to spice preference)
  • Ginger:
    • 1-inch piece
  • Tamarind pulp:
    • 2 tablespoons
  • Chaat masala:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin powder:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Black salt:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Salt:
    • to taste
  • Water:
    • 3-4 cups
  • Sugar:
    • 1 tablespoon (optional, adjust to taste)

    For the Filling:

  • Boiled potatoes:
    • 2 medium-sized, mashed
  • Boiled chickpeas:
    • 1/2 cup
  • Tamarind chutney:
    • as needed
  • Green chutney:
    • as needed
  • Chaat masala:
    • to sprinkle
  • Salt:
    • to taste
  • Red chili powder:
    • to taste
  • Chopped onions:
    • optional
  • Sev (crunchy gram flour noodles):
    • optional

    Instructions:

    For the Puri:

  • Mix semolina, all-purpose flour, salt, and baking soda in a bowl.
  • Add water gradually and knead to form a stiff dough.
  • Cover the dough and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  • After resting, knead the dough again and divide it into small balls.
  • Roll each ball into a small, thin disc using a rolling pin.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan.
  • Fry the rolled puris in batches until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  • Remove from oil and drain excess oil on paper towels. Let them cool.

    • For the Pani:

  • Blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, ginger, and tamarind pulp into a smooth paste using a blender.
  • Transfer the paste into a large bowl. Add water, chaat masala, cumin powder, black salt, regular salt, and sugar. Mix well.
  • Adjust the consistency and seasoning according to taste.
  • Chill the pani in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.

    • Assembling Pani Puri:

  • Make a small hole in the center of each puri.
  • Stuff each puri with mashed potatoes and chickpeas.
  • Add a little tamarind chutney and green chutney into each puri.
  • Sprinkle a pinch of chaat masala, salt, and red chili powder on top.
  • Dip each filled puri into the chilled pani and serve immediately.

Enjoy the burst of flavors with each crunchy, tangy, and spicy bite of homemade pani puri!
