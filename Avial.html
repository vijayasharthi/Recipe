HOW TO MAKE THE AVIAL AND THEIR INGREDIENTS LISTED ARE

Avial is a delicious South Indian dish known for its vibrant colors and flavors. Here's a traditional recipe along with the ingredients you'll need:

Ingredients:

  • Assorted Vegetables:
    • Typically, avial includes a mix of vegetables like carrots, beans, potatoes, drumsticks, pumpkin, etc. (You can choose based on availability and preference.)
  • Coconut:
    • Grated or shredded coconut, about 1 cup.
  • Green Chillies:
    • 2-3, adjust according to your spice preference.
  • Yogurt:
    • 1 cup, beaten well.
  • Curry Leaves:
    • A sprig.
  • Cumin Seeds:
    • 1 teaspoon.
  • Turmeric Powder:
    • 1/2 teaspoon.
  • Salt:
    • To taste.
  • Coconut Oil:
    • 2 tablespoons.
  • Mustard Seeds:
    • 1 teaspoon.
  • Curd:
    • 1 cup.

    Instructions:

  • Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Wash, peel (if required), and chop the vegetables into uniform-sized pieces. Traditionally, they are cut into long pieces.

  • Cook the Vegetables:
    • In a pot, add the chopped vegetables with turmeric powder, salt, and enough water to cover them. Cook until the vegetables are tender but still firm.

  • Prepare Coconut Paste:
    • Grind coconut, green chilies, and cumin seeds to a coarse paste without adding water.

  • Mix Coconut Paste and Vegetables:
    • Once the vegetables are cooked, add the coconut paste to the pot. Mix well.

  • Add Yogurt:
    • After a couple of minutes, add the beaten yogurt to the pot. Stir gently to combine. Be cautious not to boil vigorously to prevent the yogurt from splitting.

  • Tempering:
    • In a separate small pan, heat coconut oil. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add curry leaves and fry for a few seconds. Pour this tempering over the avial.

  • Serve:
    • Your avial is ready! Serve it hot with rice or enjoy it as a side dish with any Indian meal.

    This recipe offers a basic guideline. Feel free to adjust the ingredients and spices according to your taste preferences. Enjoy your delicious avial!
