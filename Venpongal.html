HOW TO MAKE THE VEN PONCAL AND THEIR INGREDIENTS LISTED ARE

Ven Pongal is a traditional South Indian dish made with rice and lentils, typically served as a breakfast or a festival dish. Here's a basic recipe along with the ingredients:

Ingredients:

  • For Pongal:
  • Raw Rice:
    • 1 cup
  • Yellow Moong Dal (split mung beans):
    • ½ cup
  • Water:
    • 4 cups (adjust as needed for desired consistency)
  • Ghee (clarified butter):
    • 3-4 tablespoons
  • Cashew nuts:
    • A handful (optional)
  • Whole black pepper:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin seeds:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Ginger:
    • 1-inch piece, finely chopped or grated
  • Curry leaves:
    • 1 sprig
  • Asafoetida (Hing):
    • ¼ teaspoon
  • Salt:
    • to taste
  • For Tempering (Tadka/Tempering):
  • Ghee (clarified butter):
    • 2 tablespoons
  • Mustard seeds:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin seeds:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Whole black pepper:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Cashew nuts:
    • A few pieces (optional)
  • Curry leaves:
    • 1 sprig
  • For Garnish (optional):
  • Coriander leaves (cilantro):
    • Finely chopped, for garnish

    Instructions:

  • Wash Rice and Dal:
    • Rinse the rice and moong dal thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.

  • Cook Rice and Dal:
    • In a pressure cooker or a pot, add rice, moong dal, water, salt, ginger, whole black pepper, cumin seeds, curry leaves, and asafoetida. Pressure cook for about 3-4 whistles on medium flame or cook in a pot until the rice and dal are soft and mushy.

  • Mash:
    • Once cooked, open the pressure cooker carefully and mash the rice and dal mixture well. If it’s too thick, you can add some hot water to adjust the consistency.

  • Prepare Tempering:
    • In a separate small pan, heat ghee for tempering. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add cumin seeds, whole black pepper, cashew nuts, and curry leaves. Fry until the cashews turn golden brown.

  • Combine Tempering with Pongal:
    • Pour the tempering over the mashed rice and dal mixture. Mix well.

  • Final Touch:
    • Add additional ghee if desired and mix well. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves if using.

    our Ven Pongal is ready to be served hot with coconut chutney or sambar! Enjoy!
