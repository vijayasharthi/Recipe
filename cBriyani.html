HOW TO MAKE CHICKEN BRIYANI

Here's a step-by-step guide to making chicken biryani:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • Whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Ghee or oil
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Fried onions (optional)

Instructions

  • Marinating the Chicken:
    • In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well until the chicken is evenly coated with the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably longer for the flavors to meld.

  • Cooking the Rice:
  • In a large pot, bring water to a boil.
  • Add soaked rice, bay leaf, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and salt.
  • Cook rice until it's 70-80% done. It should still have a slight bite to it.
  • Drain the rice and set aside.


  • Making the Biryani:
  • In a heavy-bottomed pan or a biryani pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat.
  • Add thinly sliced onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown and caramelized.
  • Add marinated chicken pieces along with chopped tomatoes and slit green chilies.
  • Cook the chicken until it's almost done and the tomatoes are mushy.
  • Sprinkle biryani masala, fried onions (if using), and half of the chopped cilantro and mint leaves. Mix well.
  • Layer half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture.
  • Sprinkle the remaining chopped cilantro and mint leaves on top of the rice.
  • Layer the remaining rice over the herbs.
  • If using saffron, drizzle the saffron-infused milk over the rice.
  • Cover the pan tightly with a lid or aluminum foil to trap the steam.
  • Cook biryani on low heat for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked and the rice is tender.
  • Once done, gently fluff up the biryani with a fork.
  • Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) and salad on the side.

    • Enjoy your homemade chicken biryani!
