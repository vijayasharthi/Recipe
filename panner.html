HOW TO MAKE THE PANNER TIKKA AND THEIR INGREDIENTS LISTED ARE

Paneer tikka is a popular Indian dish made with marinated paneer cubes grilled or baked to perfection. Here's a recipe along with the ingredients you'll need:

Ingredients:

For Marinating Paneer:

  • Paneer (Indian cottage cheese):
    • 300 grams, cut into cubes
  • Hung curd (thick yogurt):
    • 1/2 cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste:
    • 1 tablespoon
  • Lemon juice:
    • 2 tablespoons
  • Kashmiri red chili powder:
    • 1 teaspoon (for color, adjust according to spice preference)
  • Garam masala powder:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander powder:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin powder:
    • 1/2 teaspoon
  • Chaat masala:
    • 1 teaspoon
  • Salt:
    • to taste
  • Oil:
    • 2 tablespoons
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves):
    • 1 teaspoon (optional)
  • Turmeric powder:
    • 1/2 teaspoon (optional)
  • Mustard oil (for authentic flavor):
    • 1 tablespoon (optional)

    For Grilling/Baking:

  • Bell peppers (capsicum):
    • 1 each (red, green, yellow), cut into chunks
  • Onion:
    • 1 large, cut into chunks
  • Skewers (if grilling)

    • Instructions:

    Marinating Paneer:

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine hung curd, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, Kashmiri red chili powder, garam masala powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, chaat masala, salt, oil, kasuri methi, and turmeric powder.
  • ix well to form a smooth marinade.
  • add paneer cubes to the marinade and gently coat them with the marinade. Make sure each paneer cube is well coated.
  • Cover the bowl and let the paneer marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1-2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld.

    • Grilling/Baking Paneer Tikka:

    Grilling:
  • Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  • Thread marinated paneer cubes, bell pepper chunks, and onion chunks onto skewers alternately.
  • Brush the grill with oil to prevent sticking.
  • Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side or until the paneer and vegetables are lightly charred and cooked through.
  • Rotate the skewers occasionally for even cooking.
    • Baking:
  • Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
  • Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or aluminum foil.
  • Place marinated paneer cubes, bell pepper chunks, and onion chunks on the baking sheet in a single layer.
  • Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until the paneer and vegetables are cooked through and slightly charred, flipping halfway through the baking time.
    • Serving:
  • Once cooked, remove the paneer tikka from the grill or oven.
  • Serve hot garnished with fresh coriander leaves and lemon wedges.
  • You can also serve paneer tikka with mint chutney, onion rings, and lemon wedges on the side.

    • Enjoy the delicious and flavorful paneer tikka as a starter or appetizer!
